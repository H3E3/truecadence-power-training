Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-29

TrueCadence is a cycling training analysis web app. We help users analyze their own training data, including activity summaries, power, heart rate, training load, FTP estimates, recovery, nutrition and training suggestions.

Data we collect

Users may upload FIT/TCX/GPX files or authorize supported third-party services to import their own cycling activity data. Parsed training summaries may include date, duration, distance, power, heart rate, cadence, training load, power curves and similar activity metrics. Users may also enter profile, goal, recovery, sleep, wellness or feedback information.

How we use data

Data is used to display training history, calculate training load and power metrics, generate analysis, provide training suggestions and support user-requested imports. Data is shown only to the authorized user inside TrueCadence, except for administrator access needed for support, debugging, security and beta operations.

Third-party integrations

For Intervals.icu and similar services, TrueCadence requests authorization only from the user who owns the data. The initial Intervals.icu OAuth scope requested is read-only activity access. We do not write, modify, delete or publish data to Intervals.icu unless a future feature explicitly asks for additional permission.

AI/ML data use

We do not use Intervals.icu data or imported third-party activity data to train, fine-tune, benchmark or build AI/ML models. User data may be processed to generate analysis for that user inside TrueCadence.

Data sharing and resale

We do not sell, resell or share user activity data with advertisers or data brokers. We do not replicate social features, routes, segments, leaderboards or community data from third-party platforms.

Retention and deletion

Raw uploaded files are kept temporarily for parsing and debugging during beta operations and may be automatically cleaned up. Parsed training summaries may be retained to support historical analysis. Users can request deletion or disconnect imported data.

Security

We use HTTPS for public access and aim to minimize stored secrets. API keys, OAuth tokens and similar credentials should be treated as sensitive. Users should revoke access if they suspect compromise.

Contact

For privacy or data deletion requests, contact the TrueCadence operator through the beta support channel or by email: kiritohekun@gmail.com.
